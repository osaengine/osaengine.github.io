## One Million Tokens: Why It Matters

Google continues to bet on long context. **Gemini 3.1 Pro** retains its record-setting window of **1 million tokens** — roughly 700,000 words, or several full-length books.

For practical purposes, this means:

- Loading an **entire codebase** of a medium-sized project in a single request
- Analyzing **annual financial reports** without losing context
- Working with **long conversation histories** and documentation
- Processing **multi-hour transcriptions** of meetings and calls

## ARC-AGI-2: Testing Abstract Reasoning

The [ARC-AGI-2](https://arcprize.org/) benchmark tests a model's ability to perform abstract reasoning — tasks that a child can easily solve but that stump most AI systems.

Gemini 3.1 Pro scored **77% on ARC-AGI-2**, one of the best results among commercial models:

| Model | ARC-AGI-2 |
|-------|-----------|
| Claude Sonnet 4.6 | 79.2% |
| **Gemini 3.1 Pro** | **77.0%** |
| GPT-5.3 | 74.5% |
| DeepSeek V3 | 71.3% |

## Market Share Is Growing

According to analysts, Google's share of the LLM API market has grown from 12% to 18% over the last six months. Key reasons:

### Pricing

Google offers some of the most competitive prices:

- **Input**: $3.50 / 1M tokens
- **Output**: $10.50 / 1M tokens
- For context >128K: $7 / $21 per million

### Google Cloud Ecosystem

Integration with **Vertex AI**, **BigQuery**, and other Google Cloud services makes Gemini attractive to enterprise clients already using Google's cloud infrastructure.

### Multimodality

Gemini 3.1 Pro natively supports:

- Text, images, audio, and video
- Code generation and analysis
- Working with tables and structured data

## What This Means for Traders

The 1M token long context opens up interesting possibilities:

1. **Loading entire trade histories** over extended periods for pattern analysis
2. **Simultaneously analyzing** multiple company financial reports
3. **Processing a full day's news feed** without missing important details

That said, the quality of working with information at the beginning and end of a long context may differ — the so-called "lost in the middle" problem.

Google is firmly securing its place among the top three AI market leaders, and Gemini 3.1 Pro is a strong argument in this race.
