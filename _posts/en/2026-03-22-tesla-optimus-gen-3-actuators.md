## Next-Generation Hands

**Tesla** continues to impress with its robotics progress. **Optimus Gen 3** features completely redesigned hands — each containing **50 actuators**, providing unprecedented dexterity for humanoid robots.

For comparison:

- **Optimus Gen 1** (2023): 12 actuators per hand
- **Optimus Gen 2** (2024): 22 actuators per hand
- **Optimus Gen 3** (2026): **50 actuators per hand**

## What This Enables

50 actuators per hand allow Optimus to:

- **Manipulate small objects** — bolts, wires, electronic components
- **Use tools** — screwdrivers, soldering irons, wrenches
- **Perform delicate operations** — assembling fragile components
- **Adaptive gripping** — automatic grip force adjustment

Tesla showed a demonstration in which Optimus Gen 3:

1. Picks up an egg without breaking it
2. Unscrews a bottle cap
3. Inserts a USB cable into a port
4. Folds a t-shirt

## The Fremont Factory

Elon Musk announced that part of the production space at the **Fremont** factory (California) will be repurposed from car manufacturing to Optimus robot production. According to plans:

- **Q2 2026**: pilot line producing 100 robots per month
- **Q4 2026**: scaling to 1,000 units per month
- **2027**: target of 10,000 units per month

Musk stated: "In 5 years, Optimus will bring Tesla more revenue than cars."
