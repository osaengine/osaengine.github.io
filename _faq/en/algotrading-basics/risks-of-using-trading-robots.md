Although trading robots offer many advantages, their use comes with certain risks. Understanding these risks will help you minimize potential losses and improve trading efficiency.

## Main risks:

1. **Algorithm errors:**
   - Incorrect or poorly tested strategies can lead to losses.
   - Unoptimized code may react incorrectly to market events.

2. **Technical failures:**
   - Loss of internet connection or server failures can stop the robot.
   - Issues with exchange APIs can disrupt trade execution.

3. **Market anomalies:**
   - Robots may not handle non-standard situations, such as sharp price spikes.
   - High volatility can lead to inadequate algorithm behavior.

4. **Human factor:**
   - Incorrect robot configuration or running without testing.
   - Neglecting to monitor the system's operation.

## How to minimize risks:

- **Test strategies:** Verify the algorithm on historical and real data.
- **Use backup systems:** Set up monitoring and automatic connection recovery.
- **Limit losses:** Implement stop-loss mechanisms and limits on maximum losses.
- **Update algorithms:** Regularly review and adapt the robot to changing market conditions.
