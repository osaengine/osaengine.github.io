Trading robots can be configured to implement various strategies. Choosing the right strategy depends on your goals, trading style, and risk level.

## Main strategies:

1. **Scalping:**
   - Short-term trades aimed at profiting from small price movements.
   - Requires high-speed market access and low latency.

2. **Trend trading:**
   - Based on identifying and exploiting market trends.
   - Often used for medium-term and long-term trades.

3. **Arbitrage:**
   - Exploits price differences of the same asset across different markets.
   - Examples: statistical arbitrage, spot-futures arbitrage.

4. **Market making:**
   - The robot simultaneously places buy and sell orders, earning on the spread.
   - Requires a high level of liquidity.

5. **News trading:**
   - Reacting to economic events or corporate news.
   - Often applied in volatile markets.

6. **Counter-trend trading:**
   - Based on identifying trend reversal points.
   - Uses oscillators and indicators to identify entry and exit points.

## Tips for choosing a strategy:

- Assess your experience level and goals.
- Start with a simple strategy to understand how the robot works.
- Continuously test strategies on historical data before launching.
