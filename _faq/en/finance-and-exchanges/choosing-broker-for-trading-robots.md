Choosing a broker is one of the key steps when working with trading robots. The right broker will ensure stable order execution, data access, and convenient interaction through an API.

## Main selection criteria:

1. **Trading robot support:**
   - Check whether the broker provides an API for connecting a robot.
   - Make sure the broker's platform is compatible with your strategy.

2. **Commissions:**
   - Study the commission structure for trades, data, and account maintenance.
   - Pay attention to spreads and hidden costs.

3. **Available markets:**
   - Make sure the broker provides access to the markets you plan to use (forex, stocks, cryptocurrencies, etc.).

4. **Execution speed:**
   - For high-frequency strategies, it is important that the broker ensures minimal delays.

5. **Regulation:**
   - Choose brokers operating under the supervision of financial regulators (e.g., SEC, FCA).

6. **Customer support:**
   - Availability of a support service for resolving issues related to robots and trading systems.

## Selection tips:

- Start with a broker that offers a demo account for testing the robot.
- Study reviews from other users to understand the level of service.
- Check whether the broker has loyalty programs or discounts for active traders.

A properly chosen broker will become the foundation for successful automated trading. Take the time to study the terms to avoid potential problems.
