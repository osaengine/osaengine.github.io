Risk management is a key aspect of working with trading robots. A sound risk management strategy helps minimize losses and ensure the stability of the trading system.

## Core risk management principles:

1. **Limiting losses:**
   - Use stop-losses for automatic exit from losing trades.
   - Set daily loss limits to stop the robot when a certain threshold is reached.

2. **Risk per trade:**
   - Never risk more than 1-2% of total capital on a single trade.
   - Calculate position size based on risk and stop-loss level.

3. **Diversification:**
   - Split capital across multiple strategies or markets.
   - Avoid concentrating funds on a single instrument.

4. **Drawdown monitoring:**
   - Regularly track the account's maximum drawdown.
   - Stop the robot if the drawdown exceeds the specified level.

5. **Leverage control:**
   - Use moderate leverage to reduce the probability of large losses.
   - Consider the broker's margin requirements.

## Tips:

- Continuously analyze trade statistics and adjust risk management parameters.
- Do not rely entirely on the robot — regularly check its performance.
- Use reserve funds to cover unexpected losses.
